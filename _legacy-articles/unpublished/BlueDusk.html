Friend or foe? Team mates as both collaborators and opponents
Shiftstoned:
Last time we met, it was really interesting to hear about the ways that Team SPG works together. Do you have a favorite collaborative deck-building story?

Tonygeeeee:
Soono made an Armory list right as he joined SPG. I told him his version was trash. I then apologized and tried the deck. It was pure gas. And I’m forever grateful God gave me Soono.

Shiftstoned:
Wow, a gift from God? What about you SooNo?

SooNo:
I dont have just one story. It's been enjoyful every time we sit down to finalize or brew from “scratch”. I get in a voice call, then listen to these absolute mad men talk about bad cards and worse ideas.

Like BruisedByGod will say, “Nostrix is god tier.” Or Tonygeeeee “Dude, Suffocate is really really good.”

Then SecondBlue, “What are you on about? Lethrai draftstarter (Duskstalker) is insane.” And Paradox, “Call me crazy, but End of the Barrel seems really good.” Now Drolicheck, “Dude, who needs Obliterate when you have Infernus?”

Finally childroland, “So guys, I havent played all week. I don't know what is good. But BruisedByGod's list seemed fun. So I'm just going to run with it.”

And in general: “Who won today?” Answer: childroland, duh.

This list goes on and on. But the best part about all this madness is we win hahaha.

Shiftstoned:
What is it like to face your teammates in a competitive event?

Tonygeeeee:
I hate it. Even when you win it feels bad.

But for me, winning isn’t a possibility anyway hahaha. I’m 0-6 vs Secondblue. And like 2-15 overall versus my teammates since the Season Two invitational .

Once, I was playing against Bradykin (Chalice vs Armory), and he missed lethal. So I joined his voicechannel and I just called him out hahaha.

SooNo:
I ask for nothing more then to face the best players on good decks. It's the only true way to test your skill and get better at anything you do.

Plus its A LOT OF FREE WINS!! ahahaha

Tonygeeeee:

Shiftstoned:
What reactions have you received from the Eternal community on Team SPG’s recent success?

Tonygeeeee:
I think most people from the community don’t care about team affiliations. But when we see people chant our names in Twitch Chat, it always feels good!

SooNo:
I dont get "reactions." People are either not too fond of me, or know I'm a scrooge and [they are] better off not talking too me hahahaha.

Shiftstoned:
Tonygeeeee, you must be proud of your teammates. What has it been like to watch the SPG organization grow and flourish?

Tonygeeeee:
When I first joined, childroland just won an invitational and the team as a whole was doing great. I feel like I grew a lot as a player just talking to players like Roland, Hithar and Paradox.

We don’t do a whole lot of content creation besides the SPG Podcast and streams. But I think most people recognises the SPG players as being some of the best in the world. It’s been very humbling to be part of this group for a little bit more than a year.

What I hate though is that now I’m considered a trash player within this organisation… Maybe I should form my team with newer players so I can feel good about myself hahaha….

Shiftstoned:
What goals do you have for SPG going forward?

Tonygeeeee:
We always talk about creating more content, but we’re mainly focused on competitive play. If DWD ever establish a competitive scene, you can rest assured we’ll be there, thriving for the top spot.

Shiftstoned:
It looks like each member of Team SPG brought versions of at least one similar deck (Praxis Tokens) to the ECL invitational. Plus BBG and SooNo brought versions of Alessi.

What contributed to both the individual and group decisions about which decks to bring?

SooNo:
After my zero merchants to camat0's about six merchants in the ETS finals the night before, BruisedByGod and I were going over options on what we want to play.

He FINALLY agreed to play Combrei for me. And I had to play his bad Valkryie deck, both out of respect.

Really we then asked each other what uninteractive, mindless aggro deck can we jam and not feel bad? Ah Praxis Tokens.

After we got our 3 we wanted to have a deck with a card we each "enjoy," or for me "hate least." His was the ugly buzzard Nostrix and mine was the glorious, amazing, unstoppable perfect Moment of Creation. We looked over the lists and reged em up.

Shiftstoned:
How is it different to prepare for a conquest format (four decks) compared to a single deck event format?

SooNo:
Honest conquest is nonsense. There are many things I dislike about it. Having four decks to play and all that jazz seems "cool," but it's all a waste and unnecessary.

Shiftstoned:
Do the cash prizes provide extra motivation? Were TGP prizes awarded in your local currency or Soylent Meal Replacement Products?

SooNo:
Cash prizes are always great. But winning is the most important part.
